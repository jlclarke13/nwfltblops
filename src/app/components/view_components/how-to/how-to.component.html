Overview

Blops is a great way to chill out, watch netflix, and then drop the hammer on somebody's shiny. While these fleets are mostly relaxed, they do require some discipline, obedience, and know-how. The Bridge Ship itself and pod implants are worth around 3 Billion. Mistakes can be expensive, but are very very avoidable.

This guide will help you understand how to fly in a blops ship, and minimize mistakes. The most important thing is to listen to your FC and be patient. Undocking or warping around when not being told to can lead to being station camped in the lightest of circumstances to accidentally decloaking the Bridge and losing it.

Preparation

  • If you have your own ship, you can put it in the blops staging system so that is it already there when pings go out.

    If you do not have your own ship, I can provide ships. Make sure you are early to the fleet so I can hand you your ship.

    If you do not already know the current staging, ping Silv R on discord.

  • When a fleet is pinged, get in your bomber, undock and cloak in a Safe.

FC Wat Do?

  • You should already be undocked and in space unless your FC say's otherwise. Make sure you are cloaked.

    Now wait for further instructions.

  • When your FC tell's you to warp to the Bridger, first ensure that you are cloaked and then warp to the bridge at 0.

    Now wait for further instructions. This could be a long wait. Be patient. If at any point you realize you've been drifting, inform the FC that you need to warp off and back. Warp away and then warp back to the bridge at 0.

  • When our Hunters find a target, they will call that they are in warp. At this point, wake up and get ready to Jump Through The Bridge. Do not decloak. Just be ready, looking at your screen.

  • Once one of our Hunters successfully tackles a target, a cyno will be lit. The bridge will decloak open a bridge and say "Take the bridge". Decloak your ship if you were not already decloaked by the bridge. Take The Bridge.

  • When you appear on grid, there will be a big shiny in front of you. Before you attack it there are some things you need to do. Align towards a celestial that is not in the direction of the target. Now you can lock up the target, turn on your target painter or disrupter, and fire your torpedos. If you take damage, warp to your align immediately. Make a bookmark on the way out. When you land, you can warp back to the fleet and align to your bookmark.

  • If the target lights a cyno, be very ready to warp. If ships come through the cyno warp immediately to your align. Make a Safe, and cloak up.

  • When we kill a target the FC will call to warp off grid. Warp to your align, and make a Safe mid warp. Reload and repair while warping and then cloak. You do not need to wait for the reload to finish to cloak. Warp to your Safe and await further instructions. If the kill site is safe, the Astero pilots will loot. Loot goes to corp as it funds SRP and ship provisions.

  • The Bridger needs to reset. Just hang out cloaked in your safe spot for now. Once the bridge resets, we will basically do what we just did all over again.

Emergencies

Emergencies are situations that require immediate action and could result in a fleet welp, or bridge loss.

  • This will decloak the Bridger and the entire fleet. Immediately get off grid. Warp to a random celestial and recloak. Wait in a safe for further instructions.

Do's and Don'ts

  • If you are unsure of something, feel free to ask. The fleet runs much better when the members know what to expect.

  • If you have something important to say that the FC needs to hear, you can loudly say, "Check Check", to clear the comms and deliver the intel.

  • Get into space up to 10 minutes before the fleet and cloak up in a Safe. This helps us get many people undocked and ready in the event that our enemy gets tipped off that we are staging. The more of us that are out in space, the less that can get station camped.

  • Warping to the Bridger when it is not ready can result in a decloak. Depending on the situation this could be bad, or absolutely terrible. Don't do this. If you are unsure as to where you should be, ask the FC.

  • If instructed to warp to the Bridger, make sure you Cloak First, Then Warp. If you cloak while in warp to the Bridger, mistakes can happen. If your cloak doesn't trigger, or double triggers, we will end up with a decloaked fleet.
